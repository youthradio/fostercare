A Journey Through Foster Care

Foster care is designed to be a temporary court-ordered living situation for children whose parents are unable to care for them (as determined by a court order) at the time. The goal of the system is to return children to a safe, permanent home (reunification, adoption or guardianship) as soon as possible. But for various reasons, some kids remain in the system for years, eventually “aging out” as adults.

Youth Radio reports on the experience of young people who grow up -- and age out -- in foster care.
